Writing Example 1

Discussion 4

Throughout history, there have always been problems with punishing criminals and what should be done to criminals after someone has been convicted of a crime. The punishment of a crime has changed over time, along with the civil rights of the criminal. The correction of criminals has always been an ongoing debate for centuries. So, how are the prison systems in the past any different from the modern-day prison system of today?

Before there was a criminal justice system of correctional institutions, prisons, reformatories, and rehabilitation institutions, there was little or no punishment for criminals. Criminals get swift punishment with no civil rights for the criminal offenders. If convicted, the sentences were cruel and quick punishment. If instituted for incarceration, convicted offenders would spend long periods of time in jail. For a person, misdeeds were typically banishment, slavery, restitution, corporal punishment, and/or execution. There was no history of any prison system till the twelfth century. At that time, King Henry II ordered the construction of county jails to hold vagrants and thieves before sentence. In the early 1300 in Florence, Italy was punishing offenders for criminal would be placed in jail cells. Offenders were separated into enclosed cells by classified gender, age, mental state, and seriousness of the crime. Where in America, in the early seventeenth century, the modern American correctional system began under the leadership of William Penn.

There was some setback in the penal institution after William Penn's death. Penn revised the criminal code to forbid capricious use of mutilation and physical punishment, and torture. A more humane penalty of imprisonment was giving as hard labor, moderate flogging, finds, and forfeiture of property. They were doing away with widely public forms of punishment, like branding irons, stocks, pillories, and gallows. Still, in 1776 Benjamin Rush, a Quaker from Pennsylvania, adopts Penn's penal code, along with formed the Philadelphia Society for Alleviating the Miseries of Public Prisons. Society would bring some orderly treatment to the growing penal system and some degree of humanity. The Quakers also factor in the economic potential of the prison industry. Over the years, some reform efforts have been adopted into the prison system. That correction experts believe with treatment, education, and training. Inmates would reform offended into model citizens. Today's prison system has changed to a more humane approach.

Today, there are more correctional facilities that house dangerous felons and maintains strict security measures, like maximum and super-maximum-security prisons. The nonviolent offender is likely in a medium-security prison where it allows freedom of movement for rehabilitation workers and other therapeutic personnel. Then there is "club fed," with tennis courts and pools, known as minimum-security prisons for more are less white-collar criminals.

Today, the correctional system not only has a maximum, medium, and minimum-security prisons, it also has alternative correctional institutions like prison farms, where prisoners work on farms that produce dairy products, grain, and vegetable crops. Another alternative is boot camps like correctional institutions. This concept is for a short period of stay, with high-intensity exercise that will "shock" the inmate into going straight.

Writing Example 2

Legalize and Medical Use Marijuana

Growing up in times were marijuana for recreational was a bad thing. Marijuana is for medical purposes for any reason was not heard of doing those times. Marijuana was a dangerous gateway drug that would lead a person to harder drugs, sure heroin and cocaine, that would lead to drug abuse. The positive externality for illegal drug dealers would be. If more people use illicit drugs, the more money a drug dealer makes. The negative externalities would be more crimes (like property thefts), violence, and anti-social behaviors.

While looking for the externalities (positive and negative) for legalizing marijuana/medical used marijuana were somewhat different than thought. According to a Newsweek article, violent crimes decrease along the US-Mexico border that legalized medical marijuana. States like Colorado and Washington legalizing recreational use of marijuana have little to no effect on the number of violent and property crimes. When it comes to medical marijuana, there are positive externalities in the medical world. On the WebMD website, there are many articles about the benefits of marijuana. Medical marijuana can help treat several conditions like Cancer, Alzheimer’s disease, Epilepsy, and Glaucoma. Along with helping people with chronic pain, nausea, and vomiting due to chemotherapy.

Legalizing marijuana and medical use marijuana makes people think about the good and bad of marijuana, especially when a person has a loved one who can benefit from its medical use.
